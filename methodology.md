# Research Methodology: Sourcing Federer Companies in Pune

## Objective
To identify 25 manufacturing companies in Pune, Maharashtra that fit the "Federer" Ideal Customer Profile (ICP): Rs.50Cr-500Cr revenue, highly differentiated products, technical decision-makers, and active growth signals.

## Why Pune?
Pune was selected due to its dense concentration of specialty chemical, precision engineering, and medical device ecosystems (specifically around the Bhosari, Pimpri-Chinchwad, and Kurkumbh industrial areas). It offers a rich blend of legacy engineering firms and modern deep-tech manufacturing startups.

## Sourcing Funnel & Tools Used

**1. Top-of-Funnel Identification (Avoiding Google Search):**
Instead of relying on generic Google searches which yield SEO-heavy service companies, I targeted regulatory registries to establish a baseline of "Differentiation" (C3) immediately:
* **Medical Device Registries:** Sourced ISO 13485 certified companies based in Pune to find high-barrier manufacturers (e.g., *ACE Medical Devices*, *Biorad Medisys*).
* **Government Defence Corridors:** Looked for MSMEs actively supplying to DRDO/Indian Navy (e.g., *Fast Track Communications*).
* **Exhibitor Lists:** Scraped past CPhI and Chemspec India exhibitor lists, filtering for Pune headquarters to isolate specialty chemical manufacturers (e.g., *Melzer Chemicals*).

**2. The Filtering Process:**
* **Auto-Disqualification via Tofler/ZaubaCorp:** To ensure adherence to the Rs. 50Cr - 500Cr revenue band and founder-led requirement, I ran the initial list against MCA data. Companies like *Clean Science* (>1000Cr) and *Aquapharm* (PE-Backed) were instantly disqualified despite fitting the technical profile.
* **"Service Company" Trap:** Cross-referenced "Life Sciences" companies against their actual output. For example, *Vimta Labs* has a major Pune presence but was disqualified as it is a Contract Research Organization (CRO), not a manufacturer. 

**3. Deep-Dive Scoring (C1-C6):**
* **Leadership (C4):** Utilized LinkedIn to verify the academic background of founders (e.g., verifying Dr. Vaibhav Tidke's PhD from ICT for *Science for Society*).
* **Growth (C6):** Analyzed the "News/Media" sections of company websites and LinkedIn job postings for capacity expansions and hiring velocity.

## Key Learnings
* **The MSME Cap is Tight:** Many of Pune's best specialty chemical and pharma companies experienced massive growth during 2020-2023, pushing them well past the Rs.500Cr revenue ceiling. Finding companies that are differentiated but still in the <500Cr band requires looking at younger startups or highly niche B2B hardware providers.
* **Subsidiary Hallucinations:** Several highly technical companies in Pune's biotech parks are actually subsidiaries of massive pharmaceutical giants (e.g., *Gennova* is backed by Emcure; *Enzene* is backed by Alkem). They must be aggressively filtered out to maintain the "Founder-led" criteria.
